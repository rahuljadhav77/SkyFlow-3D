Gravity yields

The first push off the earth is the hardest. Here, in the lower atmosphere, the air is thick with the scent of pine and the hum of life. The world below begins to shrink into a map of memories.

0 – 1 000m
02

Into the Clouds

Silver veils drift across the sun. The brilliant blue gives way to a soft, heavy grey as we enter the heart of the cloud layer. Here, the world is muted, a silent kingdom of mist and moisture.

1 000 – 5 000m
03

The Burning Horizon

As we crest the weather, the sky ignites. A sea of orange and gold stretches to the edge of the world. The clouds below are now a glowing carpet, catching the final warmth of the day.

5 000 – 20 000m
04

The First Light

The amber fades into a deep, velvet indigo. One by one, the ancient stars appear—unblinking and sharp in the thin air. The chaos of the world below is replaced by the eternal order of the cosmos.

20 000m+

The Exosphere

Above all else,
vision remains.

From this height, boundaries vanish. The storm and the calm are one. A reminder that perspective is the greatest gift of the ascent.
